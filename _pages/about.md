I was born as one of a set of triplets in Israel, where I was the odd one out.



## Childhood

As a child, I dabbled in various arts. 

I mailed the most famous children’s author in Israel books with fan-fiction that I wrote. 

I created a theatre show that I performed every year from kindergarten to middle school. 

I played the piano for six years. 

I was in the school choir, and opened the graduation show with a solo. 

I painted every week and created costumes. 

But I never fit in, nor knew how to. 



## Adolescence

Everything changed in middle school, where I was bullied and alone. Constant peer pressure and homophobia led to depression. I quit my hobbies, studied Japanese, and started to hide my thoughts and emotions. 

Coming out in high school to my close friends didn’t help. I focused on homework, and gave up on the idea of finding love. To my confusion, I also became bisexual. I started writing my first novel, inspired by my self-harm. 



## University

After ignoring everyone’s career advice, I graduated summa cum laude in a multidisciplinary degree course in Humanities and Arts from Tel-Aviv University. 

It made me clinically depressed at first, and ended as one of the most fun and eye-opening things I’ve experienced. 

Then I graduated with merit from the UK’s most prestigious Creative Writing master’s program. It also triggered an existential crisis, followed by cutthroat fun. 

I was about to start a PhD and stay in the UK for good, when COVID and money thwarted it. 



## Big Trip

I returned to Israel with my dream crushed. Nothing I tried bore fruit. I was stuck in an environment that suppressed my identity, and didn’t leave room for growth.

After two and a half years, I lost the will to live. I decided that I deserved to be happy, and started to take down the walls I had built in middle school. 

When Japan reopened to tourists, I travelled there for three months. This extended into fourteen months across East Asia, as the war in Israel turned me into a refugee. I documented each day of my trip on this website. 

My travel blog helped me open up and share my shy, private self. I literally came out of my shell by posting my childhood journals. 

I became a new person - confident, adventurous, extroverted. A mature evolution of my childhood self.

At the same time, I experienced love again, and again, and again. A misfit robot had left Israel, and ended a sociable romantic in Asia.



## Present

Relocating to Tokyo was the next step for me. I attended a Japanese language school for a year. Nowadays, I navigate corporate slavery, while branching into music and comedy.
